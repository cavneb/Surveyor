URUG Rails Workshop Instructors Summer 2009


Jeremy Israelsen

Jeremy has been programming in Ruby and Rails since 2005. He currently works as a software developer at Lead Media Partners and is pursuing his graduate degree in Computer Science. He also likes to spend his free time teaching Information Modeling at Neumont University.
Eric Berry

Eric is a convert to the Ruby language, along with Rails. He was a co-founder of ShareAPhoto.com which was acquired by Hewlett-Packard in 2006. Over the past two and a half years, Eric has focused on learning as much about the Ruby language as possible. Eric is the author of the O'Reily Rails Pocket Reference.

Eric has developed enterprise solutions such as Reservation Counter, an online hotel booking engine, and most recently Crop Chocolate, a 'deal of the day' site which is focused on scrapbooking supplies.
Ben Mabey

Ben has been working with Ruby professionally since 2005 and is currently a Senior Software Engineer at Lead Media Partners in Salt Lake City. He is part of the core Cucumber (acceptance testing framework - cukes.info) team and is an active member of the RSpec community. The majority of Ben's open source efforts are directed toward these communities in the form of patches, auxiliary libraries and tools, and helping out on the mailing lists and IRC channels (as 'mabes'). He has a technical blog at benmabey.com where you can see an occasional post about BDD. He is known as 'bmabey' on github and twitter.
David Richards

David Richards has been writing software for about 13 years. He started working with Ruby and Rails in 2005. He is currently working on a Blackboard killer at Instructure and likes writing analytical and machine learning code in his spare time. You can find him muttering online at blog.teguhub.com.
Brad Midgley

Brad has been working with Ruby on Rails since 2005 and is currently a software engineer at Surgeworks.
